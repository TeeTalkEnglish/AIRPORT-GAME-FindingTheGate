✈️ Airport Adventure

Scenario 2: Asking for Directions to the Gate 🗺️

📖 How to Play

This is a pair activity! One student is the Passenger and the other is the Airport Staff.

👉 The Passenger covers the LEFT side with a book.
👉 The Staff covers the RIGHT side with a book.

Ask each other questions to get the information you need! 🗣️

The passenger needs to find their gate. They ask a staff member for help.

📚 Key Phrases for Asking Directions

"Excuse me, could you tell me how to get to Gate ___?"
Polite way to ask for directions
"Am I in the right terminal?"
Check if you're in the correct building
"How long will it take to walk there?"
Find out the distance
"Is there a faster way to get there?"
Ask about moving walkways or shuttle buses
"Where can I catch the shuttle bus?"
Find transport between terminals
"My gate has changed. Can you help me?"
When your gate number changes
"Do I have enough time to get there?"
Check if you need to hurry
"What should I look for on the way?"
Ask about landmarks to help find your way

📖 Key Vocabulary

Moving walkway
A flat escalator that helps you walk faster
Shuttle bus
A small bus that travels between terminals
Terminal
A building in the airport for departures/arrivals
Information desk
A place where you can ask for help
Duty-free shop
A shop where you buy things without tax
Gate
The area where you wait and board your plane
Boarding
The process of getting on the plane
Departure board
A screen showing flight times, gates, and status
